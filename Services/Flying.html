About Flying Pest Control

Flying pests are insects that use wings to travel, often causing health hazards, structural damage and significant discomfort to occupants. Mosquitoes transmit disease, wasps and hornets can trigger life-threatening allergic reactions, and flies contaminate food and surfaces. Flying pests breed rapidly and populations can explode within days. Our treatment covers both the active insects and the breeding sites to ensure the infestation does not simply return.

Flies reproduce rapidly and often breed in hidden areas, so DIY solutions may only provide temporary relief. Our professional fly exterminators can help eliminate exterior fly populations and prevent future problems.

With MPN Holdings fly pest control, homeowners get expert service backed by proven methods and experienced technicians who understand fly behavior.

How We Treat Flying Pests

We select the most appropriate treatment method based on the pest species, severity of the infestation and the type of property. Below are the three primary methods we use.

  • Fogging and Misting Treatment

    Best for: Quick elimination of large infestations

    Fogging and misting release a fine insecticide mist into the air, reaching hidden areas where flying pests breed and rest. This method is highly effective for flies, mosquitoes and other airborne insects, especially in severe infestations.

  • ULV (Ultra-Low Volume) Treatment

    Best for: Long-lasting control in homes and businesses

    ULV treatment uses very fine droplets of insecticide dispersed in low volumes, allowing deep penetration into cracks, ceilings and indoor spaces. It is a modern, efficient method for controlling flying insects with minimal chemical usage.

  • Fly Baits and Traps

    Best for: Ongoing prevention and eco-friendly control

    This method uses attractants to lure flies into traps or baited areas where they are eliminated. It is ideal for maintaining control after an initial treatment or for customers who prefer low-chemical solutions.

Top 3 Signs of Flying Pests

  • Clusters of Flies in One Area

    Flies gathering near windows, light fixtures, trash cans, drains, or pet areas often indicate a nearby food source or hidden breeding site.

  • Larvae or Maggots

    Finding maggots in trash cans, drains, pet waste areas, or decaying organic material is a clear sign that flies are actively breeding on your property.

  • Persistent Buzzing or Increased Activity

    Continuous buzzing sounds, especially near windows or walls, can indicate flies are trapped indoors or entering in large numbers.
