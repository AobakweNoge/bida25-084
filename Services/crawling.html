About Crawling Pest Control

Crawling pests are insects and arthropods that scuttle, walk or creep rather than fly, often invading homes and businesses for shelter, moisture or food. Cockroaches spread bacteria and allergens, termites cause catastrophic structural damage and bed bugs cause severe sleep disruption and psychological distress.

Crawling pests are typically nocturnal, meaning infestations can reach severe levels before they are noticed. DIY solutions rarely eliminate the colony and may only push the infestation deeper into the property. Our professional pest exterminators treat the root of the problem, not just the surface.

With MPN Holdings crawling pest control, homeowners and businesses get expert service backed by proven colony-level treatment methods and experienced technicians who understand crawling pest behaviour.

How We Treat Crawling Pests

We select the most appropriate treatment method based on the pest species, severity of the infestation and the type of property. Below are the three primary methods we use.

  • Gel Baiting

    Best for: Quick colony elimination of cockroaches and ants

    Cockroach and ant gel bait is applied directly to harbourage sites — behind appliances, inside wall voids and along skirting boards. The bait is transferred back to the colony by worker insects, causing a cascade effect that eliminates the entire colony within days.

  • Residual Spraying

    Best for: Chemical bug elimination

    Residual spraying involves applying long-lasting insecticides to surfaces where pests are likely to travel or hide. This method provides extended protection and is effective against a wide range of crawling pests.

  • Soil Termiticide Treatment

    Best for: Permanent prevention of termite re-entry

    A liquid termiticide is applied to the soil around and beneath the property, creating a continuous chemical barrier that termites cannot cross. This method protects the structure permanently and is the industry standard for subterranean termite control.

Top 3 Signs of Crawling Pests

  • Droppings or Smear Marks Along Walls

    Cockroach droppings resemble dark coffee granules and are found in corners, inside cupboards and along skirting boards. Bed bug droppings appear as small dark stains on mattress seams and bedding.

  • Mud Tubes or Hollow-Sounding Timber

    Subterranean termites build pencil-width mud tubes along walls and foundations to travel between the soil and the timber they feed on. Timber that sounds hollow when tapped is a strong indicator of internal termite damage.

  • Live Pest activity

    The visible movement of pests such as cockroaches, ants, and bed bugs inside a property. Seeing them on floors, walls, or in hidden areas—especially during the day—indicates an active, established infestation that requires immediate treatment.
